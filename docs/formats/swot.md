# 🧭 SWOT Analysis Prompt Guide for Enterprise Evaluation

This guide provides structured prompts to evaluate any system, initiative, project, feature, vendor, or risk using the **SWOT** (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) framework.

---

## 🟩 Strengths  
> Internal advantages or assets that give a competitive edge.

**Prompts:**
- What are the most valuable or unique assets this has?
- What does this do better than competitors or past solutions?
- What internal resources (tech, team, process) are helping it succeed?
- Where have we consistently performed well in relation to this?
- What evidence supports this being a trusted or efficient option?

**Example:**  
"What specific technical features give our internal platform a performance edge over vendor alternatives?"

---

## 🟥 Weaknesses  
> Internal limitations or issues that hinder success.

**Prompts:**
- What recurring problems or bottlenecks are associated with this?
- Where have we historically struggled with this type of work?
- Are there any known gaps in capability, reliability, or coverage?
- What dependencies could create failure or friction internally?
- How sustainable or maintainable is this approach over time?

**Example:**  
"What skillsets are we lacking in the team that could prevent effective use of this tool?"

---

## 🟦 Opportunities  
> External chances to improve outcomes, grow, or gain value.

**Prompts:**
- What upcoming trends or changes could be leveraged?
- Are there underexplored use cases or markets we could enter?
- Can this solve adjacent problems or scale to new teams?
- What partnerships, integrations, or investments would unlock more value?
- How might this improve compliance, security, or cost control?

**Example:**  
"What external compliance changes in our region could create demand for this system?"

---

## 🟨 Threats  
> External risks, disruptions, or emerging competitors.

**Prompts:**
- What external forces (regulatory, economic, market) could hurt this?
- Are competitors investing in similar features or pricing models?
- What emerging technologies could make this obsolete?
- Could this introduce security, privacy, or reputational risk?
- Are vendor lock-in or platform dependencies a concern?

**Example:**  
"How could a shift in our cloud provider’s pricing model undermine the value of this approach?"
