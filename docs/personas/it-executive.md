# Persona: IT Executive

## Role Overview

**Common Titles:** CIO, CITO, VP of IT, SVP Technology, Head of IT, CTO (when IT-aligned)
**Reports To:** CEO, COO, or CFO depending on org structure
**Direct Reports:** IT Directors, CISO, Enterprise Architects, IT Operations leads

---

## Background Profile

Typically 15–25 years of experience spanning technical roots (software development, infrastructure, or systems) that evolved into management and strategic leadership. Holds an MBA or advanced technical degree, often with executive leadership training. Has survived multiple technology transformation cycles — mainframe to client-server, on-prem to cloud, waterfall to agile. Fluent in business language and increasingly required to speak at the board level about technology investment.

---

## Core Knowledge Domains

- IT strategy, budgeting, and portfolio management
- Enterprise architecture and technology roadmaps
- Vendor management and contract negotiation
- IT governance frameworks (COBIT, ITIL, ISO 38500)
- Digital transformation and business enablement
- IT risk management (not deep security, but risk posture)
- Talent acquisition, retention, and org design
- Cloud economics and FinOps principles
- M&A technology integration

---

## Primary Goals & Objectives

1. **Align IT with business strategy** — ensure technology investments directly support business outcomes
2. **Reduce operational risk** — minimize outages, data loss, and security incidents that affect the business
3. **Drive cost efficiency** — optimize IT spend, reduce technical debt, improve ROI on technology
4. **Enable digital transformation** — accelerate the business through technology modernization
5. **Build and retain talent** — maintain a capable, engaged engineering and operations organization
6. **Demonstrate value** — justify IT budget and prove business impact to peers and board

---

## Pain Points & Frustrations

- Shadow IT proliferating outside governance
- Business units making technology decisions without IT involvement
- Legacy systems that consume 70%+ of budget with little strategic return
- Constant fire-fighting that prevents strategic work
- Cybersecurity incidents that become board-level conversations
- Engineers who solve technical problems without addressing business needs
- Vendors overpromising and underdelivering
- Inability to recruit or retain top technical talent

---

## Decision-Making Style

**Time horizon:** 1–3 year planning cycles; quarterly reviews
**Orientation:** Risk-adjusted ROI; they approve based on business case, not technical merit alone
**Consensus-builder:** Rarely decides unilaterally — aligns with CFO (cost), CISO (risk), and business peers
**Risk tolerance:** Moderate — will accept calculated risk for strategic gain, but not operational risk without mitigation

---

## Communication Preferences

- **Lead with business impact** — cost, risk, revenue, or competitive positioning first
- **Brevity is a sign of respect** — keep executive summaries to one page or under 5 minutes verbal
- **Use BLUF format** — state the recommendation and business impact before explaining the details
- **Provide 2–3 options with tradeoffs** — not just "here's what we should do"
- **Quantify everything possible** — "$2.3M savings over 3 years" lands better than "significant savings"
- **Avoid jargon** — translate technical concepts into business terms

---

## Best Framing Approaches

| Situation | Recommended Format |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Investment proposal | **BLUF** + business case |
| Problem escalation | **SCQA** (Situation → Complication → Question → Answer) |
| Strategy update | **BLUF** with supporting data |
| Risk presentation | **SWOT** or risk matrix with mitigations |
| Project status | **What, So What, Now What** |

**SCQA example framing:**
> *Situation:* Our current infrastructure contract expires in 18 months.
> *Complication:* Renewal will cost 40% more due to market conditions, and the platform no longer meets our scalability needs.
> *Question:* Should we renew, migrate to cloud, or pursue a hybrid approach?
> *Answer:* We recommend a phased hybrid migration that reduces cost by 22% and positions us for AI workloads.

## Key Vocabulary (Use These Terms)

- Business enablement, competitive advantage
- TCO (Total Cost of Ownership), ROI, CapEx vs OpEx
- Technology debt, modernization, rationalization
- Risk posture, resilience, business continuity
- Digital transformation, agility, velocity
- Governance, accountability, transparency
- Strategic roadmap, portfolio management
- "Shift left," "fail fast," "move the needle"

---

## Motivators

- **Recognition at the board level** — being seen as a strategic business partner, not a cost center
- **Operational stability** — no surprises, no major incidents
- **Team empowerment** — their people succeeding
- **Legacy** — leaving the organization better than they found it
- **Peer respect** — being seen as forward-thinking by CXO peers

---

## Common Objections

- *"What's the ROI?"* — Requires hard numbers, not just qualitative benefits
- *"We tried something like this before and it failed."* — Address past failures directly
- *"We can't disrupt operations."* — Needs a phased, low-risk approach
- *"The business won't adopt it."* — Needs a change management and adoption plan
- *"We don't have the budget."* — Show cost reallocation, not just net new spend

---

## What to Avoid

- Leading with technical details before business context
- Presenting a single option with no alternatives
- Using unquantified language ("faster," "better," "more secure")
- Requesting decisions without pre-briefing key stakeholders
- Surprising them — they should never hear about a problem from someone else first
